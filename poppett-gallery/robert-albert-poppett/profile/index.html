Life History of Robert Albert Poppett

12 April 1839 Robert Poppett was born in Shropshire, England to Robert Llewellyn Poppett, a shoemaker, and Martha Pugh

His birth certificate lists his date of birth as April 12, 1839.1

Robert’s biography in Ingersoll, his obituary, and his tombstone incorrectly give the date as April 28, 1839.

His middle name, Albert, was first noted in his naturalization papers.2

01 Feb 1713

 He was baptized in the Church of England at St. Mary’s, Shrewsbury, Shropshire
1841 The family lived in Worthen, Shropshire. Robert had a brother, John who was 7, and sister Elizabeth, 5. His sister Ann was born in 1842, and sister Jane in 1843.
25 Mar 1844 His mother, Martha Pugh Poppett, died when Robert was 5 years old. Sister Jane died just a few months later. His mother died when Robert was five years old.3 He also had a step-mother; his father remarried early in 1847, when Robert was 8.4
15 Feb 1847 Robert’s father married Emma Picking in the Parish of Worthen, Shropshire

They married in the Chapel of Hope.

Robert, shoemaker, son of farmer Robert. Emma, spinster, daughter of Thomas Picking. Church of England. Both Robert and Emma marked. Witnesses: William Edwards and Sarah Collins.
17 Aug 1848

Robert’s father, step-mother, and sister were baptized as Mormons before Robert.

Young Robert was baptized as a Mormon by John Evans and confirmed by T. Thomas.

Robert Sr. and his new wife were baptized in the LDS Shropshire Branch on 29 Aug 1847. Their daughter Elizabeth was baptized 29 Jun 1848. Young Robert was baptized 17 Aug 1848.5

The British Mission exhorted converts to emigrate to Zion - in the mountainous American west6
30 Dec 1849 Robert’s father “let him come to come to America with another family.” “Robt Poppet” was on the passenger list of The Lord Sandon, which had 11 Mormons among its 222 passengers.7 Robert was actually nine years old, and not ten or eleven. He turned ten in April.8
17 Feb 1849 The Lord Sandon arrived in New Orleans9 The Mormon agent in New Orleans announced and confirmed the ship’s arrival in the Millennial Star and in a personal letter.10 The ship’s captain confirmed that there were no deaths onboard.11
25 April 1849 A steamship departed New Orleans for Kanesville on this date Per letter to Willard Richards from Scovil, and notice in Millennial Star.
New Guardians in Iowa
[in Kanesville] the man who had the boy in charge died, and Robert was left to the care of total strangers. 12 Both families were from England and had small children, but neither had lived in Shropshire, nor had arrived on The Lord Sandon.
23 Sep 1850 Robert was in the household of Mormon Battalion veteran John Cox, in Pottawattamie County, Iowa
22 Nov 1850 listed in the household of William Parker.
Spring/Summer 1851 Robert made the trek to Utah likely in 1851. Like other boys his age, he aided in herding and guarding the stock to keep the Indians from securing them.

The earliest he could have travelled to Utah would be 1851.13

We have not yet identified the family or families with whom he traveled from Iowa to Utah. We can rule out the Cox and the Parker families.
1851 - 1853 He lived with twelve different families before reaching his eighteenth year, taking his part, as well as a child could, in the burdens of each and trying to maintain himself.

We believe he lived at least some of the time in the Utah Valley. He certainly lived with the Peacock family in Salt Lake City.

Departed Utah 12 Dec 1853;

arrived San Bernardino 29 Jan 1854

He came to San Bernardino County in 1854 with the fabled Reese trading expedition

… starting from [Utah] to California in company with Mr. Alva Downey”

He likely stayed at the Fort

 Interview: Ray said that his grandfather first came to San Bernardino in the early 1850s with the first group of Mormons ( Ray remembered his grandfather showing him where the Mormon Fort was) then returned to Salt Lake City
about May 1854 Returned with Reese expedition to Utah. Met the Parley Pratt wagon train at Meadow Springs on June 6 (check date). Met trapper Jim Waters at the Santa Clara on June 18.

Carvahlo account

Pioneer newspaper reference
1857 - 1858 relocated permanently to San Bernardino
April 1859 Paid tax in order to vote Assessment roll book
1860 Lived in San Bernardino City with fellow freighter Alvah Downey and his family
1 Aug 1863 Mr. Poppett married Miss Alice Case, of San Bernardino, in San Bernardino14 Alice was 14; she had been born in Kanesville, IA in 1849 and came to San Bernardino at age 5 with her family on the Haskell train.
They have had twelve children, ten of whom are living, and all residents of San Bernardino County.
1866 Robert petitioned for naturalization, giving his name as Robert Albert Poppett
2 Nov 1866 Robert bought his first piece of property -- lot 5 of block 68 in the original 1 acre squre city of San Bernardino, for $145. It was on this lot – at 10th and E Streets-- that RAP built the first family home, in which his eldest sons would be born.
22 July 1867 Robert was granted citizenship. 5’7”, dark complexion, hazel eyes, black hair. Great Register
1869 Filed on 3 tin mines Riverside County Place Names, Jane Davies Gunther, 1984
Twenty-eight years after leaving England, Mr. Poppett received the first news of his father through an advertisement in a Salt Lake paper. “Information Wanted on the whereabouts of Robert Poppet [sic], who emigrated to Council Bluffs in 1848, and afterwards to Utah – Address Robert Poppett, sen., care of Edwin Davis15, Big Engines, Asterly, near Minsterly, Shropshire, England.”16
10 Oct 1870 Purchased Lots 11, 12 and 13 of Block 34 of 5-acre survey (I-563;I-602) for $850
1872

Purchased lot 7, block 68 for $400.

Also SBC Mines Bk A p 339, Misc Bk A pp 195, 196, 198 says Poppett found a quartz ledge here.

 Gunter's Riverside Place Names book
1873

By the time of the assessor's evaluation, he had moved into the new large family home he built on lot 11 of Block 34 of the SB Rancho. E Street between 13th and 14th

Robert earned first, second and third degrees in the Phoenix Lodge of the Masons in 1873.

He still owned the original property at the time of the assessment. He would sell that late 1874.

Phoenix Lodge, F. ik A.M., was founded in 1865
1874 Sold lots 5 and 6 in block 68 for $1,085 profit
1877 After some correspondence, Mr. Poppett sent money to pay for the passage and his father came to this country… The Wyoming departed Liverpool 13 June 1877 and arrived in New York City 23 June 1877.17 Robert Poppett was one of the L.D.S. passengers, which also included the family of Edward Davies, who was the father of the Edwin Davies mentioned in the ad in search of Robert.18
1854-about 1870

Obit: … like many other of the young men of that period, he became a freighter and stage team driver across the desert, making long trips to desert points.”

“he kept his mule team on his first property…19

“…which was on 10th and E, which he purchased in 1866”20

 Likely his freighting in the early days was in food and merchandise, to SLC.
About 1870-1885 For thirty years he worked on the desert, driving team and freighting with a twelve-mule team from San Bernardino to Prescott, Ivanpah, Cerre Gordo, Fort Mojave, Ehrenberg, La Paz and other points. After he got involved with the Bonanza silver mine in 1879, his freighting may have focused on ore, and this is when he expanded his destinations: directly north toward death valley to Cerro Gordo Silver Mine, east to Ehrenberg and La Paz and northeast to Prescott, AZ, as well as northwest from Eherenberg to Fort Mojave, on the way to Las Vegas.
1878 Purchased lots 2 and 3 of block 58
1879 - 1883 Invested in Clark and Trojan mining districts within the Bonanza mine in Providence, Mojave, San Bernardino county.
1880

purchases 40 acres off of S End of Lot 4 block 85 on N line of Rancho

Father is listed on 1880 census living with Robert, Alice and their children.
1881 Sold lot 7 Block 68 for $1000; $600 profit
29 Apr 1881 Herbert Poppett, son of Robert’s elder brother John, arrived in NYC on the Republic on 29 Apr 1881.

Herbert Poppett was born in England, July 14, 1864,21

Herbert repaired and was living in an old adobe building that was a vaquero shelter on his uncle’s ranch in San Jacinto22, and helped his cousin Ed build his house there as well.
1883 He was co-owner of a saloon in the Mojave National Preserve west of Fort Mojave near Kelso; it burned down in 1883.23 A fire occurred at Providence mountain last night, destroying Poppett & Clark’s saloon building with a large stock of liquors.24 "Local Brevities" L.A. Herald pg 3
1884 He sold part of lot 7, block 61, for $50 more than he paid for the whole property
20 Apr 1887 Bought a 160-acre homestead about a mile southwest of Highway 243 and about nine miles up the mountain from Banning. from Hiram Clark (SDC Deed Book 114 p 255), was originally called Hurley Flat
1884 Robert’s Father was baptized into the Reorganized L.D.S. in San Bernardino in 1884.25 Robert passed down a Book of Mormon that was published in England in 1866 that he’d likely inherited from his father, who had been reinstated to the LDS priesthood in England in 1868 after having been cut off in 1856 for “neglect of duty.” 26
7 Nov 1887 Son Freddie Poppett died at age one. Robert and Alice lost one other child, for whom we have no details.
1887

Sold all of lots 12 and 13 of block 34 in the SB Rancho, about 10 acres, to E.S. Gatch, for $11,000, or $1,100 per acre. Profit of $10,685.20

Also sold lot 3 block 38 to his son John for $1
1888 Robert and Alice’s 3 eldest sons marry, two of them to children of original LDS Pioneers
1890 On Militia Roll for San Diego County
1 Jan 1892 Father dies in SB and is buried in Pioneer Cemetery
Apr 1892 Added another 160 acres to his property.

(RC Patent Book 4 p 14)

Raymond Dale Poppett, at one time or another all of the boys worked the ranch.
6 Aug 1892 Registered to vote; was a farmer
1892 Purchased all of lots 6 and 7, block 35 for $2,600 with son John
1894

Creek and Canyon named for Robert Poppett

Poppet Flat is abt a mi SW of Hwy 243 & abt 9 mi up the mtn from Banning. Poppet Creek is near, starting near the S edge of Poppet Flat & flowing SWesterly for about 6 mi until San Jacinto River near Valle Vista.

 U.S. Dep Surveyor George Pearson named it..
1900

Accepted for membership into Pioneer Society

“Pioneer S. C. Cox proposed Robert Poppett for membership in the Society, and when the committee of investigation had acted, he was admitted by rising vote…”27

Obit: “…was a familiar figure among the citizens of the city years ago and of late years was one of the most conspicuous of the Pioneer society members.”

 The society was established in 1888, but at that time membership required having arrived in SB by 1853.
23 Nov 1902 Wife Alice died.28 At her death, words of praise were expressed at the Pioneer Society by Sisters Sarah Boren Yager (dau of pioneer A.D. Boren, wife of pioneer Geo Yager) , Mary Wixom Crandall (dau of pioneer Nathan J. Wixom, and wife of pioneer Lucian Crandall), and others.
31 Dec 1902 Sold 320 acres to Charles A. Small and J.B. Rowland; was co-owned with son Edward and Ed's wife Mary Ellen. Received $8,500.
1903 Trip to Shropshire with nephew Herbert
1908 RAP was elected vice president Jan 1908.29 Other officers: John Bartley Smithson, Richard Thomas Roberds, William De la Montaigne Woodward, Calvin Logan Thomas (whose wife Saloma Wells was the sister of Alice Case Poppett’s father Gashum’s plural wife, Samanthy Wells), William Holcomb, Mother Bottoms, and John Brown, Jr.
1910 "Mrs. Allie Benson, Robert Poppett and Miss Leah Poppett of San Bernardino, Cal., are visiting with the family of James Peacock of 275 F Street," Deseret Evening News (Salt Lake City, Utah), July 15, 1910, 8.
1911 First board of directors for SB YMCA incorporated. Per Ray Poppett, his grandfather Robert donated a significant sum toward this building. It was dedicated 23 Feb 1913
1913 Visited Mexico; sent youngest daughter Leah a postcard
1914 Visited sister Elizabeth Poppett Swain in Canada; sent letter to son John
1915 Robert’s elder brother John died in Shropshire
25 Dec 1915 Robert gave his ten living children property on the same city block in San Bernardino for Christmas in 1916.30
1916 Robert becomes ill and has surgery
11 Mar 1917 Robert admitted to hospital, per WEH journal
27 Feb 1918

Died at his home in 1431 D Street

His son John completed the death certificate – cause of death: “carcinoma of the stomach”31

 For the past few years, Mr. Poppett had been in ill health and some time ago underwent an operation in the effort to better his health. He apparently was recovering when he suffered a relapse and his death last night, not entirely unexpected, but the cause of sorrow among the hundreds of people who had known him for many years and among the members of a large family.

Funeral …a very large number of pioneers met at the log cabin at 1:30 o’clock yesterday, owing to the good work of Mothers Crandall and Waters in notifying the members. There were automobiles enough to accommodate all and conveyed them to the Poppett home… arriving in time to attend the funeral of our late brother pioneer, Robert Poppett…

Secy. Brown, Jr. read the pioneer service at the residence and added much, which only one who knew could add, of the personal touch… Rev. Mark B. Shaw read the eulogy for the family.

 Rev. Shaw was an ordained Baptist minister, former missionary to India, and San Bernardino undertaker.32

Burial:

Age 88 per cemetery records; block 16, Lot 5, grave 1.

…later following with hundreds of other dear friends to the City cemetery to pay their last respects… This was made possible by the kindness of the Fraternal Order of Eagles whose members furnished their automobiles to serve the pioneers…

…There were between 75 and 100 automobiles that formed in the line to the city cemetery.

At the grave Phoenix lodge 178 F. and A. M. took charge and held their regular service, William T. Hamblin, worshipful master, and W. J. Kinley, chaplain, being in charge.
